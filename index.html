The Raw Material

Exact words that reveal what prospects actually need.

01

"I have dumb questions about QuickBooks... what is [this]?"

What This Means:

Shame about finance knowledge gaps. She's embarrassed to ask. The real pain isn't QuickBooks—it's feeling stupid about money while running a company.

02

"Calmness is important in the CFO thing. I've surrounded myself with calm folks."

What This Means:

The buyer is seeking emotional safety, not just technical competence. They want someone who won't panic when everything's on fire.

03

"What's Brian gonna do?" / "Challenging transitional period"

What This Means:

Fear of transition disruption. Not "will this work?" but "will this break what we have?" Trust in team continuity matters more than features.

04

"They thought it was way too expensive... but finding staff is hard."

What This Means:

Price objection is actually capacity objection. Not "can we afford this?" but "can we afford NOT to solve our hiring problem?"

05

"Likelihood it's not going to be one of the three packages."

What This Means:

Standard packages feel like force-fits. Customization isn't a nice-to-have—it's a requirement. They need to feel heard, not slotted.

The Hidden Pains

Everyone knows about "month-end close." These are the pains nobody talks about.

The Shame of Not Knowing

Founders feel stupid asking "dumb questions" about finance. They nod along, then Google later.

Funnel Response: Normalize gaps. "Most founders at your stage ask the same thing."

The Transition Terror

"What's gonna happen to our current person?" Fear of change > pain of current state.

Funnel Response: Address continuity explicitly. Show the bridge, not the cliff.

The Push/Pull Anxiety

"How hard do I push without being pushy?" Sales uncertainty creates paralysis.

Funnel Response: Give permission. "Take the time you need. Here's what clarity looks like."

The Staffing Impossibility

"Finding staff is hard." They can't hire their way out. Need solution, not headcount.

Funnel Response: Team-as-a-service. "You get depth without the hiring nightmare."

What Actually Works

The specific behaviors, phrases, and moments that advance deals.

1

The Personal Rapport Investment

Script:

"How's Carmel? Couldn't be happier to be home or wish you were still there?"

2

The Momentum Acknowledgment

Don't pitch to a problem. Celebrate their success, then offer to enable more.

Script:

3

The Generous Connection

Offer value before asking. Introductions, resources, help. Positions Route One as partner with network, not vendor with invoice.

Script:

"Absolutely, I'll introduce you to John. He's perfect for this."

4

The Behavioral Credential

Not titles. Not schools. Specific behavioral descriptors that paint a picture.

Script:

"Incredibly capable, smart, meticulous, and calm."

How This Changes Everything

The funnel shouldn't diagnose books. It should diagnose chaos.

OLD APPROACH ❌

  • • Lead with "accounting services"
  • • Ask about "books" and "reports"
  • • Show three packages
  • • Discuss price early
  • • Technical credibility

NEW APPROACH ✅

  • • Lead with "operational calm"
  • • Ask about "sleep" and "drowning"
  • • Build custom solution together
  • • Calculate founder time cost first
  • • Emotional safety + technical

Specific Funnel Changes:

Question 1: Not "What's your revenue?" but "How's your current finance setup handling your growth?"

Reveal: Founder time calculator. Hours × Rate = Hidden cost. Immediate value demonstration.

Credibility: Mitch/Dean/John bios with behavioral descriptors, not titles. Show calm, not credentials.

Pricing: Never lead with price. Always calculate savings first. $2,300 = "saves you $5,200"
